Earning a Degree with a Sensory Impairment

95 percent of information about the world around us comes through our senses, specifically through sight and hearing. A sensory disability is where that ability to retrieve information is taken away due to loss of vision or loss of hearing.

According to the Office of Disease Prevention and Health Promotion, at least 1 in 6 Americans have a sensory or communication impairment. These people are our friends, family members, neighbors, and co-workers. We all dwell in the same world filled with light and sound. College Life: Earning a Degree with a Sensory Impairment seeks to explore the lives of two college students with sensory disabilities working to earn their Bachelors degrees at a public university in Virginia. Through their stories, we can experience and connect with the lives of people who live in this information-driven world without the senses of vision and hearing.

Access

Read More

Various organizations across campus provide services to make college life accessible to students with sensory disabilities. Students with sensory disabilities may register with the Office of Disability Services in order to get accommodations in the classroom and for class materials. The Virginia Department for the Blind and Vision Impaired (DBVI) is a state organization that works in coordination with the university. DBVI representatives travel to JMU to work with visually impaired students who need help learning to safely navigate campus. Sign language classes are also offered each semester, providing students the opportunity to learn more about the visually impaired community. The elective classes help to make sensory disability a more common part of the public sphere on campus.
