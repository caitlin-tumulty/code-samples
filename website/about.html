Brooke

My name is Brooke Bogin and I am a senior Media Arts and Design major at James Madison University. Working on the accessibility aspects of this project has opened my eyes to a complex field I never knew existed. I have learned so much about the ways that people in this field have developed innovative technology to make life more manageable for individuals with sensory disability. I hope that people who visit this site will leave with an increased sense of understanding and admiration for people dealing with sensory disability, and for the people who devote their lives to helping them.

Cassidy

My name is Cassidy Harvey and I’m a senior Media Arts & Design major at James Madison University. My time with C.J. destroyed a lack of empathy and information I had regarding sensory impairments. Because of this, I thought it was necessary to share a glimpse of C.J.’s life with others so they might find empathy and learn ways to adapt to their peers with sensory impairments. I was honored that this was my final project of college and I hope that those who visit this site leave with a new perception of a world without senses.

Elaina

Hi! My name is Elaina Taylor and I’m a senior double majoring in Writing, Rhetoric and Technical Communication and Media Arts and Design at James Madison University. This project has introduced me to fascinating data sets from the U.S. Census Bureau regarding persons with disabilities, which I played around with using data visualization tools. I learned so much while working with some amazing teammates and awesome subjects in making this project a reality.

Molly

My name is Molly O’Toole and I am a senior Media Arts and Design major at James Madison University. I wanted to do this project because my younger sister is visually impaired and is getting ready for college. I wanted her to be able to learn from students that have a similar condition and how they are able to handle college life. My time with Scarlet has opened my eyes to everything that is possible for my sister in college. I am so happy to have found an amazing role model for my sister and one that will hopefully help her see how amazing college can be.

Skye

I am so thankful to have had an enriching experience learning of sensory disabilities in relation to life in college. There was so much to be discovered, not only by us as a team but for so many others who may not have any idea about the process. Thank you all participants for your time (even when we seemed annoying) and my team for being so efficient, fun and hard workers! Enjoy.
